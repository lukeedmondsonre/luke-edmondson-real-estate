Why People Are Moving to Chico, CA — And What to Know Before You Do

From Bay Area transplants to remote workers leaving Sacramento, Chico is drawing people in. Here's what's behind it — and what the real estate market looks like when you arrive.

Northern California is seeing a real shift in who is buying homes and where. People from San Francisco, San Jose, Los Angeles, Sacramento, and even Seattle are making their way to Chico — and the reasons driving that migration are remarkably consistent.

What's Pulling People to Chico

The short answer: quality of life at a price that still makes sense. Chico offers something increasingly rare in California — a genuine community with real amenities, outdoor access, a walkable downtown, and home prices that don't require a seven-figure income to participate in.

Bidwell Park — one of the largest municipal parks in the United States — runs through the heart of the city. There's a vibrant downtown with local restaurants, breweries, and shops. CSU Chico brings energy and culture. And the pace of life here is genuinely different from the metro areas that most relocators are leaving.

Remote work made this possible at scale. When you don't have to commute to a Bay Area office, paying $1.2 million for a 1,200-square-foot house stops making sense. Chico, about 90 miles north of Sacramento and 180 miles from the Bay Area, puts you close enough to visit but far enough to live differently.

Bay Area vs. Chico — The Numbers

Bay Area (San Jose / Oakland)

Median home price: $1.1M–$1.4M+. Small lots, dense neighborhoods, extreme competition. Most buyers put 20%+ down just to be competitive.

Chico, CA

Median home price: ~$467K. Room to breathe, real neighborhoods, and a market where a prepared buyer can still win without giving everything away.

For many Bay Area sellers, the equity from their home sale covers the Chico purchase outright — or leaves them with a mortgage payment a fraction of what they were paying. That financial reset is life-changing for a lot of families.

What the Chico Market Looks Like for Relocators

If you're coming from a major metro, Chico will feel refreshingly manageable — but don't mistake that for easy. Well-priced homes in desirable neighborhoods still move quickly and can attract multiple offers. Being out-of-area adds a layer of complexity: you may not be able to tour in person on short notice, and you're navigating a market you don't know yet.

That's exactly where having a local agent matters. I work with relocating buyers regularly, and the process looks a little different — video walkthroughs, honest assessments of neighborhoods and value, and making sure you understand what you're buying before you commit from a distance. The goal is always the same: get you into the right home with full confidence.

Neighborhoods That Tend to Attract Relocators

  • The Avenues — historic character, tree-lined streets, walkable to downtown. Very popular with buyers coming from urban areas who want that neighborhood feel.
  • Canyon Oaks / Foothill Park — upscale, views, newer construction. Attracts buyers looking for a premium finish in a quieter setting.
  • California Park / Northwest Chico — family-friendly, good schools, newer builds with more space. Popular with families relocating from suburban areas.
  • Meriam Park / Doe Mill — walkable, mixed-use neighborhood with a village feel. Great for those who want to be close to everything.

Good Things to Know for an Out-of-Area Buyer

Don't buy in Chico without understanding the specific neighborhoods. The city varies — some areas are more established, some are appreciating faster, some have characteristics like flood zones that matter for insurance and maintenance. What you see on Zillow from 500 miles away doesn't tell you that story.

A first call with me costs you nothing and gives you a real picture of what to expect. We can sit down virtually or in person whenever you're ready — no pressure, just information you can actually use.
